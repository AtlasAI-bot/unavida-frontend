#### **Sample Cards**

**Front:** "What is the first-pass effect?"  
**Back:** "Metabolism of a drug by the liver and GI wall before it reaches systemic circulation. Occurs with drugs given PO. Can significantly reduce bioavailability (e.g., nitroglycerin <10% bioavailability PO vs. sublingual)."

**Front:** "CYP3A4: Major substrates & common inhibitors"  
**Back:** "Substrates: statins, calcium channel blockers, benzodiazepines, antihistamines. Inhibitors: ketoconazole, grapefruit juice, amiodarone. Inducers: rifampin, phenytoin."

**Front:** "Dosing adjustment in CKD Stage 4 (GFR 15-29): Decision approach?"  
**Back:** "1) Identify drug elimination route. 2) If >30% renal elimination → reduce dose or extend interval. 3) Monitor renal function regularly. 4) Avoid if possible (dialyzable alternative?). 5) Consult pharmacist if unsure."

- Progress tracking (new, learning, reviewing, mastered)
- Image-supported cards (ADME diagrams on back of cards)

#### **Case Study Structure (Each Case)**

1. **Patient Presentation** (100-150 words)
   - Demographics, medical history, medications, chief complaint
   
2. **Clinical Question** (50 words)
   - Specific nursing question related to pharmacokinetics
   
3. **Question Set** (3-5 progressing questions)
   - Assessment question
   - Analysis question (apply PK concept)
   - Decision question (nursing action)
   - Rationale & learning outcome for each

4. **Reference Links**
   - Linked section(s) in Chapter 2
   - Related videos
   - Relevant quiz questions

#### **Sample Case Study (CS-2: Drug Interaction Alert)**

**Scenario:**  
Patient: 62M with hypertension on atorvastatin 40mg daily. New diagnosis: athlete's foot → prescribed ketoconazole 200mg BID.

**Question 1 (Assess):** "What is the mechanism by which ketoconazole affects atorvastatin metabolism?"  
*Expected answer: Ketoconazole inhibits CYP3A4; atorvastatin is a CYP3A4 substrate → increased atorvastatin levels*

**Question 2 (Analyze):** "Predict the clinical consequence of increased atorvastatin levels in this patient."  
*Expected answer: ↑ risk of myopathy/rhabdomyolysis (statin side effect); monitor for muscle pain, weakness, ↑ CK*

**Question 3 (Decide):** "What is the best nursing action?"  
*Expected answer: Notify provider; recommend alternative antifungal (e.g., topical terbinafine) or statin dose reduction; patient education on muscle symptoms*

### INTERACTIVE TOOLS

#### **1. Dose Adjustment Calculator**
- **Input:** Drug name, renal function (GFR/CrCl), hepatic function (Child-Pugh)
- **Output:** Recommended dose adjustment with rationale
- **Features:**
  - Common drugs in pharmacology courses
  - GFR calculation from creatinine/age/weight
  - Confidence level (high/moderate/low—consult pharmacist if low)
  - Links to drug-specific guidelines

#### **2. CYP450 Interaction Checker**
- **Input:** Two or more drugs
- **Output:** Potential interaction, severity (minor/moderate/severe), mechanism
- **Features:**
  - Database of major CYP substrates, inhibitors, inducers
  - Color-coded severity
  - Recommendation (monitor/adjust/alternative)

#### **3. Drug Absorption Rate Comparison**
- **Interactive slider:** Select route of administration
- **Visualization:** Time-to-peak graph updates in real-time
- **Learn outcome:** Understand why route selection matters

#### **4. Pharmacokinetic Curve Plotter**
- **Input:** Half-life, dose, frequency
- **Output:** Animated curve showing drug accumulation to steady state
- **Learn outcome:** Visualize 5 half-lives principle; steady-state concept
